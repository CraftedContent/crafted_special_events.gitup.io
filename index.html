Speaker Bios

Michael S. Horton is a prominent American theologian and the J. Gresham Machen Professor of Systematic Theology and Apologetics at Westminster Seminary California. Known for his contributions to Reformed theology, Horton’s work emphasizes the doctrines of grace and the centrality of Christ in salvation.

R. Scott Clark is a distinguished American theologian and professor of Church History and Historical Theology at Westminster Seminary California. He specializes in Reformed theology, particularly the Reformed confessions and the theology of John Calvin.

Dorothea Wendebourg is a renowned German theologian and church historian, known for her expertise in Reformation history and ecumenical theology. She serves as a professor of Church History at Humboldt University in Berlin.

Dr. Ashley Null is a distinguished theologian and church historian, specializing in the English Reformation and the theology of Thomas Cranmer.

Jonathan Linebaugh is a respected theologian and New Testament scholar, specializing in Pauline studies and Second Temple Judaism. He is an Associate Professor of New Testament at Cambridge University.
